Q: What is the YRS Program? What will my child achieve through YRS?

A: YRS, which stands for Youth Research Scholars, is a growing STEM outreach program that engages students in an immersive STEM setting through guided scientific research projects under the mentorship of high school and college students. By encouraging an approach that asks questions about the natural world, the YRS program works to build an innovative mindset that is capable of organizing and applying scientific concepts in methods that lead to new questions and discoveries. Your student will grow academically as they challenge themselves to build new connections in the scientific universe, but they will also learn how to organize their time, execute procedures, and professionally present their projects at fairs and symposiums.

Q: How does the YRS Program work?

A: Every week, students will have the opportunity to directly meet and learn from experienced mentors via the after-school YRS program. Over time, students will learn the expansive foundations of building a self-directed research project, from correctly formatting a scientific presentation to lab safety to designing professional and engaging posters.
